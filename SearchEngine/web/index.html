Frequently Asked Questions

How does the search engine work?

Our search engine uses TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency) algorithm to rank documents. It analyzes the frequency of search terms in documents and compares them across the entire index to determine relevance.

Why are some words ignored in my search?

Common stop words (like "the", "a", "an", "and", etc.) are automatically filtered out to improve search accuracy and speed. This is standard practice in information retrieval systems.

Can I search for exact phrases?

Yes! The search engine processes your query and matches individual terms. For best results with phrases, include all key terms from your phrase in the search.

How are documents indexed?

Documents are automatically indexed when placed in the data folder. The system tokenizes the content, removes stop words, and builds a searchable index for fast retrieval.
